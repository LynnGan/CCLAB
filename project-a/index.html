[ Modori, モドリ ]

Scientific Name:

Memoria modoriensis

Discovered at:

In the corner of an old bookshelf inside of an abandoned library in Kyoto, 2007.

Family:

Memoria — a family of small, memory-associated ethereal beings.

Habitat:

Quiet, neglected corners: behind fridges, atop dusty books, inside old glass jars. Spaces filled with leftover words and memories.

Appearance:

Soft, translucent, pale blue like a dew bubble. Gently glows, floats slightly above the ground. Always seen cradling a glowing glass bottle filled with "phrases"

Key Characteristics:

  • Memory Guardian - Remembers and preserves spoken-but-forgotten phrases.
  • Echo Spirit - Repeats the words that no one else heard.
  • Corner Companion - Hides in peaceful corners of daily life
  • Emotion Sensitive - Glows more brightly when it senses deep human feeling

What we know so far about Modori?
Modori is a small, semi-translucent, bubble-like organism that appears exclusively in quiet, neglected spaces associated with human presence. It demonstrates an affinity for environments imbued with unspoken emotions and forgotten speech. The species is consistently observed carrying a faintly luminous glass vessel containing “verbal fragments” — unfulfilled promises, unanswered confessions, and statements overlooked or dismissed. Behavioral studies indicate that Modori is non-verbal and avoids direct interaction, yet it exhibits a pronounced sensitivity to human speech and emotion. The intensity of its internal luminescence appears to fluctuate in response to sincere or emotionally charged words. Anecdotal evidence suggests that, in periods of complete stillness, the vessel occasionally emits faint auditory echoes of these preserved phrases, though no measurable mechanism has yet been identified.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature
Modori is extremely shy and non-aggressive. It should be approached with slow, calm movements and low noise levels. Physical attempts to touch or capture the organism will cause immediate retreat or dissipation of its form.

Speaking softly and sincerely in its presence has been observed to draw Modori closer. It shows particular sensitivity to emotionally significant speech. Maintaining a peaceful and contemplative environment encourages the creature to linger. Loud arguments, anger, or forced commands will cause it to vanish.

The glass vessel carried by Modori contains stored verbal fragments and must never be handled. Disturbance is hypothesized to cause stress or loss of collected phrases.
